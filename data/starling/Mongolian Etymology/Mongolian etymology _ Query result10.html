Proto-Mongolian: *bul-
Altaic etymology: Altaic etymology
Meaning: dreggy, muddy, turbid
Russian meaning: мутный, грязный
Written Mongolian: bulaŋgir (L 133)
Middle Mongolian: buluŋgir (HY 53), bulanɣir (MA)
Khalkha: buliŋgar
Buriat: bulangir
Kalmuck: buĺǝŋgǝr
Ordos: bulaŋgir, buliŋgir
Comments: KW 30. Mong. has a number of similar forms with -a-: balar 'turbid', balbi- 'to become dirty, sullen', bala 'snuff', balǯira- 'be wet, soaked'. These may reflect a contamination of *bul- with another root, see *balai 'blind; dark'. The assumption of Mong. bulaŋgir being borrowed from Turkic (cf. MTurk. bulɣančaq, derived from bulɣa- 'to stir up') see in Щербак 1997, 110; on the other hand, Mong. is certainly the source of Tat. bolaŋɨr 'muddy, cloudy' etc.
Proto-Mongolian: *bul-
Altaic etymology: Altaic etymology
Meaning: swelling, lump
Russian meaning: опухоль, выпуклость
Written Mongolian: buluw, bulduruw (L 134, 136: bulu, bulduru)
Middle Mongolian: bulu 'hob' (HY 18)
Khalkha: bulū, buldrū
Buriat: bula, bulū; bulxaj- 'be convex'
Kalmuck: bulǝ
Dagur: bol, bolo 'bolster' (Тод. Даг. 127)
Comments: KW 59. An expressive root with numerous derivatives (bul-t-, bul-č-, bül-t- etc.); there also exist variants with -a- (bal-t-, bal-č- etc.). Most of these forms can also be found borrowed in modern Turkic and Tungus languages (see, e.g., Doerfer MT 73). Bur. > Russ. Siber. buldurun 'кочка' (Аникин 140).
